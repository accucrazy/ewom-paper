CONCEPTUAL MODEL

Eight Variables of AI-Generated eWOM

From eWOM Literature

1. Message VolumeAmount of brand-related information; visibility and cognitive processing potential
2. CredibilityPerceived trustworthiness, competence, and honesty of the source
3. ValenceEmotional attitude — positive, negative, or mixed — toward the brand

Mehrabian & Russell, 1974; Petty & Cacioppo, 1986; Hung et al., 2023

Extended for AI-Generated eWOM

4. Analytical ThinkingLogicality, rigor, and hierarchical organization in expression
5. Authenticity"Sense of personal real experience" conveyed by the text
6. OriginalityUnique, non-repetitive, non-templated content
7. Product EvaluationDegree of specific assessment of quality, design, cost-effectiveness
8. PersonaCoherent synthetic identity — traits, expertise, behavioral patterns

Pennebaker et al., 2014; Boardman et al., 2022; Shanahan et al., 2023

TECHNICAL APPROACH

Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) &
Reinforcement Fine-Tuning

Our AI-generated eWOM was produced through a rigorous SFT pipeline with reinforcement learning — not simple prompt engineering:

1

Proprietary Data

Human-verified eWOM database from internal marketing operations — eliminating organic noise

2

Internalization

Non-verified insights are internalized rather than directly quoted — ensuring original synthesis

3

Generalization

Cross-brand and cross-category adaptability while maintaining persona consistency

4

Strategic Labeling

Latent writing strategies and tactical angles encoded as training signals — learning implicit persuasion patterns

SFT vs. PROMPT ENGINEERING

❌ Prompt Engineering Only

Generic outputs, inconsistent persona, limited domain depth

✅ SFT + Reinforcement Fine-Tuning

Persona-consistent, domain-aware, human-like quality verified by HITL

High-quality eWOM deployed in
live commercial operations

ANALYSIS STRATEGY

Statistical Methods

Bivariate Pearson Correlations

Assess pairwise correlations among the 8 primary variables → identify the interconnected nature of message characteristics

📈

Multiple Linear Regression

Predict Authenticity → Originality (β=.46), Product Eval (β=.21), Valence (β=.12), Credibility (β=.12)

⚖️

One-Way ANOVA

Differences across 3 sectors (Fashion / Beauty / 3C) on all 8 variables

Discussion Note — Contextual Data Mirroring: Lower scores in Fashion and 3C reflect real-world social media dynamics — inherent market engagement benchmarks, not model limitations. The fine-tuned model faithfully mirrors the linguistic patterns of its target market.
